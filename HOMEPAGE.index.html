CURRENT SUBJECTS

  • PHYSICAL ACTIVITIES TOWARD HEALTH AND FITNESS 3 (PHATFIT): This course emphasizes the importance of physical activity and wellness. We will explore various types of physical activities, their benefits for health and fitness, and strategies for incorporating exercise into a balanced lifestyle. We will also examine the physiological and psychological aspects of exercise.
  • COMPUTER MEDIATED COMMUNICATION (CMC): This course explores the ways in which technology has transformed communication. We will examine the impact of digital platforms like email, social media, instant messaging, and online forums on language, communication styles, social dynamics, and relationships. We will also discuss the ethical and societal implications of computer-mediated communication.
  • TECHNICAL WRITING (TECWRITE): This course focuses on the principles and practices of technical writing. We will learn how to write clear, concise, and accurate documents for technical audiences. We will explore different types of technical documents, such as reports, proposals, manuals, and instructions.
  • LIVING IN THE IT ERA (LITE): This course explores the impact of information technology (IT) on our lives. We will examine the ways in which IT has transformed our work, leisure, communication, and relationships. We will also discuss the ethical, social, and economic implications of living in an increasingly digital world.
  • KONTEKSTWALISADONG KOMUNIKASYON SA FILIPINO (KOMFIL): This course examines the principles of effective communication in various contexts. We will analyze how language use varies depending on the situation, audience, and purpose. We will also explore the role of language in shaping social interactions and cultural identities.
  • FOREIGN LANGUAGE I: This course introduces students to the fundamentals of a foreign language. We will focus on developing basic communication skills in listening, speaking, reading, and writing. We will also explore the culture and society associated with the target language.
  • ELT APPROACHES AND METHODS (EAM): This course focuses on the theory and practice of teaching English as a foreign language (EFL). We will explore various approaches to EFL instruction, including communicative language teaching, task-based learning, and content-based instruction. We will also examine different teaching methods and techniques, such as grammar-translation, audio-lingual, and direct methods.
  • LANGUAGE OF LITERARY TEXTS (LLT): This course delves into the linguistic features of literary texts. We will analyze how authors use language to create meaning, evoke emotions, and engage readers. We will examine aspects such as diction, syntax, figurative language, and narrative structure. We will also explore the relationship between language and literary genres.
  • ENGLISH LANGUAGE TESTING AND ASSESSMENT (ELTA): The principles and practices of English language testing and assessment. We will examine different types of language tests, including proficiency tests, placement tests, and achievement tests. We will also discuss the criteria for effective test design, the principles of validity and reliability, and the ethical considerations in language assessment.
  • ENGLISH SYNTAX (ENG SYN): This course provides a comprehensive introduction to the structure of the English language. We will examine the rules that govern how words are combined to form phrases, clauses, and sentences. We will explore concepts such as parts of speech, phrase structure, clause types, and sentence transformations.

WHAT'S THE IMPORTANCE OF LIVING IN THE IT ERA FOR ME?

As a young person navigating this dynamic landscape, I find myself deeply impacted by the opportunities and challenges it presents. One of the most significant benefits of the IT era is the democratization of knowledge. With a few clicks, I can access a vast library of information, from academic journals to online courses, expanding my understanding of diverse subjects and fostering a lifelong love of learning. This access to information empowers me to become a more informed individual, capable of making well-informed decisions and contributing meaningfully to society. And, the IT era also presents challenges. The vast amount of information available can be overwhelming, making it crucial to develop critical thinking skills to discern credible sources and navigate the digital landscape responsibly. The constant connectivity can also lead to distractions and a sense of information overload, requiring us to develop healthy digital habits and prioritize our well-being. Moreover, living in the IT era is a complex and multifaceted experience. While it offers incredible opportunities for learning, connection, and innovation, it also presents challenges that require careful consideration. By embracing the power of ICT and using it responsibly, I can navigate this dynamic world and contribute to a brighter future.

HOW DOES ICT HELPS STUDENTS LIKE ME?

ICT, or Information and Communication Technology, has become an integral part of my learning journey, making it more convenient, efficient, and engaging. It's like having a digital toolkit that helps me access resources, collaborate with others, and manage my time effectively. One of the most significant benefits of ICT is the ease of access to information. I can find tons of information and resources online, making research and studying way more efficient. ICT also makes communication with classmates and teachers. I can connect with them through emails, group chats, and online platforms, making collaboration on projects and getting feedback effortless. This is particularly helpful for group projects, as it allows us to work together efficiently, regardless of our physical location. And, ICT has transformed the learning experience for students like me. It has made learning smoother, more flexible, and more engaging, empowering me to succeed in my academic journey.
